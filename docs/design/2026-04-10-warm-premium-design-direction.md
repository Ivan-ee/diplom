# Warm Premium Design Direction

## Design statement

Victoria Tort should feel like a premium local confectionery brand with strong digital taste:

- as clear as Apple in hierarchy and restraint;
- as commercially legible as a strong modern e-commerce store;
- warmer, softer, and more tactile than a tech brand;
- visibly real, not AI-generic.

## Brand character

Use these four adjectives as the main filter for all design decisions:

- calm
- refined
- appetizing
- trustworthy

If a solution is flashy, synthetic, overly futuristic, or generic “premium SaaS”, it is wrong for this product.

## Visual language

### Color

Base palette:

- milk white
- warm ivory
- champagne beige
- soft oat
- graphite brown for text

Accent palette:

- caramel / dusty toffee for primary actions
- muted pistachio or berry only as small secondary accents when tied to actual product content

Rules:

- one main accent, not a rainbow system;
- use color to guide choices, not to decorate every surface;
- the products themselves should provide the richest colors on the page.

### Typography

Recommended direction:

- replace the current generic pairing with a stronger premium hierarchy;
- use one modern, clean sans for body and controls;
- optionally use one restrained editorial accent face for short hero or testimonial moments only.

Suggested implementation direction:

- primary sans: `Manrope`
- editorial accent, optional and sparse: `Cormorant Garamond`

Rules:

- no serif in dense forms or utility UI;
- no oversized decorative typography that hurts readability;
- use tighter, cleaner type ramps and fewer competing weights.

### Spacing and rhythm

The storefront should feel generous, but not hollow.

Recommended rhythm:

- desktop section spacing: `80-112px`
- mobile section spacing: `48-72px`
- component gaps: tighter than today on mobile
- content blocks should feel intentionally grouped

Rules:

- never leave large empty beige or white zones without informational purpose;
- mobile layouts should collapse into shorter, more useful sections;
- premium should come from precision, not whitespace alone.

### Surfaces

Use surfaces to organize hierarchy:

- primary surfaces: soft warm white
- secondary surfaces: subtle tinted neutral
- borders: thin, low-contrast, warm gray
- shadows: soft and rare

Rules:

- avoid making blur the main brand signature;
- use radius consistently but not excessively;
- cards should feel crafted, not inflated.

Recommended radius direction:

- page-level hero media: `28-36px`
- cards and summaries: `20-24px`
- pills and segmented controls: `9999px` only when the interaction clearly benefits

### Imagery

Imagery is a first-class design material in this product.

Rules:

- use real product photos wherever possible;
- crop to emphasize frosting texture, decoration detail, and form;
- avoid placeholder blocks in premium conversion zones;
- let gallery media feel tactile and delicious, not merely informative.

### Motion

Motion should support hierarchy and quality perception.

Use:

- staggered reveals for product grids or content blocks;
- smooth state transitions for selectors and summaries;
- subtle parallax or float only if it reinforces the hero product.

Avoid:

- motion-heavy gimmicks;
- long spring animations for form flows;
- decorative animation that competes with product content.

## Page north stars

### Homepage

Purpose:

- orient new visitors fast;
- present the bakery as premium and credible;
- direct users into either `catalog` or `constructor`.

Design behavior:

- hero must feature a strong visual product or crafted environment, not an empty placeholder;
- “popular products” should feel merchandised and desirable;
- trust blocks should be concise and elegant;
- the constructor CTA should feel like the signature feature, not a generic promo block.

### Catalog

Purpose:

- make scanning fast;
- make the assortment feel curated;
- reduce effort in choosing where to click next.

Design behavior:

- filters should read as premium controls, not raw utilities;
- cards should emphasize photo, category, name, and price in a stable order;
- click hierarchy inside the card must be explicit.

### Product page

Purpose:

- remove hesitation;
- make customization obvious;
- move the user toward cart.

Design behavior:

- gallery should feel premium and tactile;
- buy box should have stronger trust and fulfillment cues;
- selectors should feel decisive and calm;
- details should be easy to access without overwhelming the main purchase action.

### Cart and checkout

Purpose:

- reassure;
- summarize;
- help the user complete the order with low cognitive load.

Design behavior:

- use a calm, guided composition;
- make order summary feel substantial and trustworthy;
- keep pickup details and payment model visible;
- reduce the visual feel of “generic form”.

### Constructor

Purpose:

- present the 3D cake as the hero experience;
- keep the control UI elegant and secondary.

Design behavior:

- the scene needs visual dominance;
- the panel should feel premium and minimal;
- step navigation should be obvious without becoming noisy;
- pricing and primary action must remain always legible.

## Anti-AI checklist

Reject any proposal that does one or more of the following:

- uses purple gradients, dark-mode gloss, or neon without brand logic;
- turns the bakery into a SaaS dashboard;
- relies on glassmorphism as the main aesthetic move;
- introduces oversized empty sections with little product value;
- uses too many pills, cards, and floating shadows at once;
- makes every block look identical;
- treats placeholder rectangles as acceptable hero media;
- makes the constructor look like a settings console instead of a premium product configurator.

## Practical definition of success

The redesign is successful if:

- the storefront feels more expensive without feeling fake;
- the catalog and PDP are easier to scan and buy from;
- the homepage has stronger product desire and stronger path clarity;
- cart and checkout feel calmer and more trustworthy;
- the constructor visually belongs to the same brand system while still feeling special.
